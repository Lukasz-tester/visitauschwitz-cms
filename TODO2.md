## 1. New Content — Highest Priority Posts

Ranking by: (search volume × current gap × time to write)

### 1.1 "How Long Does Visiting Auschwitz Take?"

**Target keywords:** "how long does auschwitz tour take", "auschwitz tour length", "how long to visit auschwitz", "auschwitz half day or full day" — all Tier 1, no current page targeting them directly.

**Why this matters beyond SEO:** Reddit and TripAdvisor are full of visitors who budgeted 2h and missed all of Birkenau. This post prevents a specific, documented regret. That protective mission = high donation intent in readers.

**Angle:** Practical time breakdowns by visit type (free self-guided PM, morning guided, study tour, private). Include a decision table. Cross-link to /tickets/, /tour/, /arrival/.

**Donation trigger:** At the end: "Planning this properly took years of guiding — condensed here so you don't waste a single hour at one of the world's most important sites."

### 1.2 "Auschwitz Photography Rules: What You Can and Cannot Capture"

**Target keywords:** "can you take photos at auschwitz", "auschwitz photography rules", "auschwitz camera rules" — Tier 1, zero competition from the site currently.

**Why this matters:** High search volume, completely uncovered by the site, and photography is emotionally loaded at a memorial — the wrong behaviour causes real offence. A guide that explains both the rules AND the ethics is unique.

### 1.3 "Where to Eat Near Auschwitz"

**Target keywords:** "where to eat near auschwitz", "auschwitz restaurant", "oswiecim food" — Tier 2, currently no competitor with high E-E-A-T covers this well.

**Why:** Visitors staying for a full day (study tour = 6h) need to eat. Nobody tells them. A licensed local guide who knows Oświęcim restaurants is uniquely credible. This is also an affiliate opportunity (booking.com restaurant affiliate).

### 1.4 "Auschwitz from Warsaw: Is It Worth a Day Trip?"

**Target keywords:** "auschwitz from warsaw", "warsaw to auschwitz day trip", "how to get to auschwitz from warsaw" — Tier 2, high volume. Currently the site has zero Warsaw content.

**Why it unlocks a new audience segment:** Warsaw visitors are different from Kraków ones — further away, likely combining with other Polish cities, may have less time. Serving this segment multiplies traffic from a different geographic source.

### 1.5 "Auschwitz Accessibility Guide"

**Target keywords:** "auschwitz wheelchair", "auschwitz disabled access", "auschwitz mobility" — Tier 2, completely unserved. The official site has minimal info; no competitor has a dedicated guide.

**Why this is strategically important:** Visitors with mobility needs have zero reliable source and maximum anxiety. A guide that solves this for them creates some of the deepest loyalty and highest donation propensity of any content type ("you're the only site that told me what I needed to know").

### 1.6 "Post-Visit: Books, Films & Resources After Auschwitz"

**Target keywords:** "what to read after visiting auschwitz", "what to watch after auschwitz", "auschwitz books" — Tier 3 volume but HIGH conversion rate for products.

**Why this is a product page in disguise:** Amazon affiliate links to Primo Levi, Elie Wiesel, Edith Eger directly monetize. A curated reading list with your guide's commentary is something nobody else provides with this authority. Natural cross-sell to a future "Pre-Visit Reading" lead magnet.

## 2. New Content — Pages (not posts)

### 2.1 Scam Operator Warning Page (new page or post, updated regularly)

**Target keywords:** "auschwitz tour scam", "[operator name] reviews", "fake auschwitz ticket site" — zero competition, high-intent.

**Why this is the site's highest-value protective content:** The Auschwitz Museum itself issued warnings about "unethical practices" of travel firms (Feb 2026). KrakowDirect and Royal Cracow have documented TripAdvisor complaints (bookings taken, visitors left at pickup with no entry). Visitors searching "[company name] scam" find nothing useful. This site can own that space entirely.

**Why it drives donations:** Someone who searched a company name, found this warning, and avoided losing €80–200 on a scam tour will be the most motivated donor on the site. Copy: "This page exists because tour companies prey on Auschwitz visitors. I name them so you don't have to find out the hard way."

**Keep it updated.** This is the only content that justifiably says "updated [current month]" in the title — freshness signals matter here.

### 2.2 Morning vs Afternoon Decision Tool (block addition on /tickets/)

Not a separate page — a structured comparison block with a simple table: Morning entry (book 6+ weeks ahead, guided only, €30–60) vs Afternoon entry (free, book 3–5 days ahead, self-guided only, closes earlier). Visitors can't find this comparison anywhere — they have to read the whole page to figure it out.

**Why:** Blocks the #1 decision confusion. The GSC data shows `/en/tickets/` gets 110k+ impressions because people are actively comparing options. A clear comparison table improves dwell time and reduces bounce.

### 2.3 /contact Page (from TODO.md)

Currently forms are embedded, no standalone page. A contact page with response-time expectations, what questions you can answer, and a small "or consider supporting the guide" nudge near the bottom.

**Why this matters for E-E-A-T:** Google increasingly values contact pages as trust signals. It also collects private guide inquiry leads (Tier 2 monetization: "15-min video prep call for €30").

## 3. Retention: Keep Visitors Longer

### 3.1 Activate the popup newsletter (code exists, not active)

**Why now:** Every month you delay, 3,500 visitors leave without an email. Email is the only channel you own — not Google, not Meta. The popup is already built.

**Expected impact:** Industry average popup conversion is 3–8%. At 3,500 visitors/month and 3% conversion = 105 new subscribers/month. At 1,200 subscribers you can run email campaigns that bring returning traffic to new posts — compounding effect.

### 3.2 Email CTA block on every page (from TODO.md)

After the donation block on each page, or separately on supplement/tour which have the highest dwell times.

**Copy angle:** Not generic "subscribe". Use: "Get my pre-visit checklist — one email, free, no spam" for /supplement/, "Get notified when ticket policy changes" for /tickets/, "Get transport updates" for /arrival/.

**Why page-specific hooks matter:** Generic "subscribe to newsletter" converts at ~0.5%. Page-specific value offers convert at 2–4x that. Each page's email offer should match what brought the visitor there.

### 3.3 Internal linking audit — cross-link the top 5 pages to each other

The /tickets/ page barely links to /arrival/. The /tour/ page doesn't link to /supplement/. Each visit to a top page should pull visitors into 1–2 related pages.

**Why:** GA shows avg 1.4–2.6 pages/session. Visitors who see 3+ pages have dramatically higher donation and email signup rates. The top pages already have the traffic — internal links are the cheapest way to increase pages/session.

**Specific high-value links to add:**

- /tickets/ → /arrival/ (after booking, "next: plan your journey")
- /tickets/ → /tour/ (after guided tour section, "see what you'll actually walk through")
- /arrival/ → /supplement/ (after transport, "what to bring and expect once you're there")
- /tour/ → /supplement/ ("before you follow this route, read what most visitors miss")
- /faq/ → every major page in the answer body

Most important. "What to bring" is a Tier 1 keyword (co zabrać do auschwitz, auschwitz dress code, auschwitz bag size). An email-gated checklist captures people at peak intent — about to travel. These are your most valuable subscribers (about to visit = most likely to read, share, and donate).

**"Annotated Map PDF"**
Unique asset nobody else offers. Offline use on-site (when visitors have no connectivity inside the camp). Gives the site a physical presence on the visitor's phone during the actual visit — brand recall for donation after returning home.

### 4.2 Email Sequence (after signup)

Currently no sequence exists. Even a 3-email sequence transforms subscribers:

2. Day 3: "The thing most visitors get wrong about Auschwitz" (links to supplement or tickets)

**Why the 3rd email matters:** The "about the author" email consistently outperforms all other conversion emails for indie sites. Readers who know who built the site convert to donors at 3–5x the rate of anonymous-site visitors.

### 4.3 Re-engagement campaign for existing subscribers

GSC shows the site has consistent traffic — there are already subscribers who signed up months ago and haven't heard from you. A single "what's new in 2026" email to existing list re-engages them, promotes new posts, and reminds them the site exists and needs support.

## 6. Technical SEO — Schema Gaps

### 6.1 HowTo schema on /arrival/

The transport steps (bus from Kraków: Step 1 get to station, Step 2 buy ticket, Step 3 board at X, Step 4 arrive at Y) are perfect HowTo schema candidates. Google displays HowTo rich results for transport queries — competitors don't have this.

### 6.2 FAQPage schema — verify all accordions have isFAQ: true

Competitor analysis found only 1 of 14 competing pages has FAQPage schema. The site's accordion system auto-generates it, but only when isFAQ is set. Check that /tickets/, /arrival/, /faq/ accordions are all flagged correctly. This affects People Also Ask appearances in Google.

### 6.3 BreadcrumbList schema

When the frontend supports it, breadcrumbs ("Home > Tickets > How to Book") improve CTR in search results by showing page hierarchy. Flag for frontend implementation.

### 6.4 Monthly opening hours table on /tickets/ or /faq/

"auschwitz opening hours" and "auschwitz visiting hours" are Tier 1 keywords. A structured table (month, open/close times, notes) on /tickets/ or a dedicated FAQ answer generates a table-rich result in Google. Currently not on the site.
