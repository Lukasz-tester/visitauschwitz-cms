Auschwitz-Birkenau Memorial is the most visited site from Krakow — and for good reason. At just 70 km away, it is an accessible day trip. But planning carefully makes all the difference between a rushed experience and a meaningful one. Here is a step-by-step guide from a licensed Auschwitz guide.

## Planning Your Day: A Realistic Timeline

7:30–8:00 — Depart Krakow (bus from MDA station or train from Główny)

9:00–9:30 — Arrive in Oświęcim, get to the Memorial

9:30–10:00 — Security, check-in, collect headsets

10:00–13:30 — Guided tour (3.5h) or self-guided visit (4–5h)

13:30–14:00 — Free shuttle to Birkenau

14:00–15:30 — Explore Birkenau

15:30–17:30 — Return to bus/train station and travel back to Krakow

Allow a full 8–10 hours from departure to return. Do not rush this visit.

3 Ways to Get There From Krakow
Bus (most popular): Lajkonik bus from MDA bus station, stands G1–G6. About 1.5 hours, 22 PLN one way. Cash or card on board, or reserve at moj-bus.pl.

Train: From Kraków Główny to Oświęcim, about 1 hour, ~15 PLN. The station is 1.5 km from the Memorial — take local bus lines 0, 2, 3, or 8 to "Muzeum I" stop.

Car: A4 motorway then DK44, about 1 hour. Parking at Auschwitz I: 20 PLN. A free lot is available 700m away.

What to Book Before You Go
Entry pass — Reserve on visit.auschwitz.org at least 2–3 weeks ahead (90-day window). Choose between a free afternoon pass or a paid guided tour. Since March 2026, all passes are online-only — no on-site sales.

Transport — Bus tickets can be bought on board, but reserving at moj-bus.pl guarantees your seat in summer.

Return transport — Check bus and train schedules for return. Last buses back fill up in peak season.

Complete booking guide.

What to Bring for the Day
Entry pass (printed or digital) and valid photo ID are required. Bring a water bottle and a light snack (no eating inside the grounds). Wear comfortable walking shoes — mostly outdoors, uneven terrain. Dress for the weather — much of Birkenau is open field. Small bag only (max 30×20×10 cm) — larger bags go in free lockers. Earphones with a 3.5mm jack for the headset during guided tours.

6 Common Day Trip Mistakes
Not reserving tickets in advance — since 2026, there are no on-site sales. Reserve online.

Arriving too late — allow at least 30 minutes before your tour slot for security screening.

Combining Auschwitz with the Salt Mines — possible but exhausting. Groups arriving at the same time cause long queues at both sites.

Not checking return transport — last buses fill up quickly, especially in summer.

Underdressing in winter — temperatures drop below zero and most of the visit is outdoors.

Bringing large bags — anything over 30×20×10 cm must go to lockers, which means extra time at security.

After the Visit
Give yourself quiet time. Many visitors find it difficult to process the experience immediately. The bus or train ride back to Krakow provides natural decompression time. Consider visiting a quieter part of Krakow that evening — Kazimierz (the Jewish quarter) or a peaceful café.
